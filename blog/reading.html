Notes on "Introduction to Evolutionary Computing"

A deep dive into the foundational concepts of evolutionary algorithms — selection, crossover, mutation, and fitness landscapes. This book provides a rigorous yet accessible introduction to the field that underpins much of modern optimization research. The chapters on multi-objective optimization were particularly enlightening for my current work.

Reflections on "The Elements of Statistical Learning"

Hastie, Tibshirani, and Friedman's classic text continues to be one of the most important references in machine learning. Rereading the chapters on model assessment and selection gave me new perspectives on the bias-variance tradeoff and how it relates to imbalanced classification problems.

2024.11.08

"Deep Work" by Cal Newport — A Review

Newport's argument for focused, uninterrupted work resonates deeply with the academic lifestyle. The practical strategies for cultivating deep work habits — scheduling, rituals, and embracing boredom — have meaningfully changed my daily routine in the lab. A must-read for anyone in research.
